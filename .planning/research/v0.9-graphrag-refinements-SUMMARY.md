# Research Summary: v0.9 GraphRAG Refinements — DRIFT Search, Path-Ranking & Incremental Communities

**Target:** `llm-agent-rag` (`github.com/costa92/llm-agent-rag`), standalone Go RAG SDK at tag `v0.5.0`
**Milestone direction:** GraphRAG refinements — the three items v0.8 named on its deferral list: DRIFT search (hybrid global-primer → local-follow-up), incremental community maintenance, and path-ranking / structured subgraph-as-evidence.
**Researched:** 2026-05-19
**Overall confidence:** HIGH on seam fit and the codebase scan (grounded in the `v0.5.0` source); HIGH on the DRIFT-as-a-third-answer-path framing (it follows directly from KG3-4 and the shipped `AskGlobal`); HIGH on the incremental-maintenance recommendation (the SDK's scale genuinely does not justify it); MEDIUM on the exact phase slice split.

---

## Executive Summary

v0.7 shipped Tier-1 lightweight GraphRAG (entity/relation extraction, `GraphStore`, `GraphRetriever` as a fourth RRF signal). v0.8 shipped Tier-3 (Louvain community detection, lazy community reports, `AskGlobal` map-reduce global search, embedding-similarity fuzzy resolution). v0.8 closed by naming three deferrals — **DRIFT search, incremental community maintenance, and path-ranking / subgraph-as-evidence** — as the v0.9 candidate set. This research evaluates all three and recommends which actually belong in v0.9.

The headline finding is **scope discipline: ship two of the three, defer the third.** DRIFT search and path-ranking are both genuine, well-shaped refinements that compose cleanly with the v0.5.0 seams and deliver user-visible value. Incremental community maintenance is **not** — it is a large, subtle optimization (correct community split/merge bookkeeping on edge changes) solving a performance problem this SDK does not have. v0.8 already made full re-detection on re-ingest the keystone (KG3-7) precisely because detection is "cheap stdlib": `LouvainDetector.Detect` is an in-memory computation over a graph whose realistic SDK-scale size is hundreds-to-low-thousands of entities. **The honest call is to defer incremental maintenance again** — not as a punt, but because the v0.8 simple approach is *correct* and *fast enough*, and v0.9's budget buys more value spent on DRIFT and paths. This mirrors v0.8's own honest call to defer DRIFT past v0.8.

**DRIFT search is the marquee v0.9 feature and it slots in with almost no architectural risk.** DRIFT (Microsoft's "Dynamic Reasoning and Inference with Flexible Traversal") composes the two answer paths the SDK already has: a global *primer* pass for broad orientation, then a local *follow-up* loop that drills into specific entities. The SDK already ships both halves — `System.AskGlobal` (the primer) and `retrieve.GraphRetriever` (the local drill-down). DRIFT is therefore a **third answer method, `System.AskDrift`**, that orchestrates the existing two — not a new retriever, not a new store capability, not a new graph algorithm. It is the smallest-surface-area, highest-value item in the candidate set, and it directly extends KG3-4's "global search is a separate answer path, not a `Retriever`" framing: DRIFT is a *third* separate answer path.

**Path-ranking / subgraph-as-evidence is the second v0.9 feature** and is purely additive to v0.7's `GraphRetriever`. v0.7's `GraphRetriever` scores *chunks* by node proximity decay (`math.Pow(decay, hop)`) and already returns a `GraphTrace`. v0.9 adds, behind a new opt-in field, deterministic ranking of multi-hop *paths* between linked entities and a structured `graph.Subgraph` (the type already exists) surfaced on the trace as evidence. `GraphRetriever`'s existing `Retrieve` contract and all its v0.7/v0.8 tests stay byte-identical — path ranking is an extra output, not a changed one.

The hard constraints hold cleanly. **v0.9 needs no new dependency, no graph database, and touches no existing seam's shape.** DRIFT is orchestration over `AskGlobal` + `GraphRetriever` + `generate.Model`. Path ranking is a pure-stdlib deterministic graph computation over the `Subgraph` the `GraphStore` already returns. Both are scripted-model / golden-test friendly. v0.9 holds the line v0.7 and v0.8 both held.

## 3. Incremental Community Maintenance — the "should this even be built?" question

**Question 2 answered, honestly: no — defer it again.**

v0.8's KG3-7 made full re-detection on re-ingest the keystone. The reasoning was explicit: community detection is "cheap stdlib", so a graph-changing re-ingest re-runs `LouvainDetector.Detect` over the whole namespace snapshot (`rag/import.go:157-172`) and `UpsertCommunities` replace-all-reconciles. The v0.9 candidate question is whether to replace that with *incremental* maintenance — updating only the communities a re-ingest actually perturbed.

**Why incremental maintenance is genuinely hard (not just "more code"):**

1. **It is a second, parallel algorithm — not a tweak.** `LouvainDetector.Detect` (`graph/louvain.go`) is a clean batch computation: build adjacency, greedy modularity moves, coarsen, repeat. Incremental Louvain is a *different* algorithm — given a small edge delta, decide which nodes to re-evaluate, and correctly handle the cases where one added/removed edge causes a community to **split** or two communities to **merge** at *every level of the hierarchy*. The literature (incremental/dynamic Louvain, DynaMo, etc.) is real but the correctness surface is large, and a wrong incremental update silently produces a community structure that batch detection would never produce — and `AskGlobal` then answers from a structure that does not reflect the graph.
2. **It breaks determinism guarantees subtly.** v0.8's KG3-6 makes detection deterministic *by construction* — sorted iteration, no randomness, golden-testable. Incremental maintenance must produce *the same* hierarchy a from-scratch `Detect` would, for *any* sequence of deltas, or the cache-hash story (KG3-2: `CommunityContentHash`) and golden tests both fragment. Proving "incremental result == batch result" across delta sequences is the genuinely subtle part.
3. **It needs a new `CommunityStore` surface.** Incremental maintenance needs to read the *prior* community assignment and the edge delta — a new method on the `CommunityStore` capability interface, implemented for both in-memory and `postgres`. That is interface churn v0.7 and v0.8 both avoided for their deferred items.

**Why the SDK does not need it:**

- **Scale.** `LouvainDetector.Detect` is an in-memory pass over a namespace graph. v0.7's KG-7 hard-caps traversal at depth 2 / fan-out 64; the SDK's realistic graph is hundreds-to-low-thousands of entities per namespace. Full Louvain at that size is milliseconds. There is no measured latency problem to optimize.
- **v0.8 already shipped the correct behavior.** Full re-detection on re-ingest is not a stopgap — it is *correct*. A re-ingest re-detects, `CommunityContentHash` invalidates only the reports whose membership actually changed (so lazy reports are *not* needlessly regenerated — `rag/global.go:356-368`), and `AskGlobal` always sees a fresh hierarchy. The cost of full re-detection is the cheap stdlib pass; the cost it *avoids* — re-summarization — is already avoided by the content-hash cache.
- **Opportunity cost.** v0.9's budget is one milestone. Spent on incremental maintenance it buys a micro-optimization with a large correctness-test surface. Spent on DRIFT + path ranking it buys two user-visible retrieval capabilities. The v0.8 research made exactly this call for DRIFT ("building global + local is already a full milestone; DRIFT is a believable v0.9"). The same discipline says: incremental maintenance is a believable v1.1+ *if a real corpus ever makes re-detection a measured bottleneck* — and not before.

**Recommendation (KG4-5): keep v0.8's full re-detection. Do not build incremental community maintenance in v0.9.** Document it in `docs/graphrag.md` as a deferred optimization with an explicit trigger condition ("revisit only if profiling shows `Detect` dominating re-ingest on a real corpus"). This is the honest call the brief asked for: the v0.8 simple approach should *stay*, and v0.9 spends its budget on DRIFT and paths.

## 4. DRIFT Search — Architecture

**Question 1 answered.** DRIFT (Microsoft Research's "Dynamic Reasoning and Inference with Flexible Traversal") is the hybrid that sits between pure-global `AskGlobal` and pure-local `GraphRetriever`. It runs a broad **primer** pass for orientation, then a bounded **local follow-up loop** that drills into the specific entities the primer surfaced, then synthesizes.

**It is a third answer path — `System.AskDrift` — not a mode or a decorator.** This follows directly from v0.8's KG3-4: global search could not honestly implement `retrieve.Retriever` because it does not return `[]store.Hit`, so it became `AskGlobal`. DRIFT has the *same* property — it produces a synthesized answer, not ranked chunks — and additionally it *orchestrates two existing answer paths*. Forcing it into `Ask` (a mode flag) or `AskGlobal` would tangle three distinct flows into one method. The clean shape is a third sibling:

```go
// AskDrift answers by a global primer pass followed by a bounded local
// follow-up loop. It is a SEPARATE answer path from Ask and AskGlobal:
// it orchestrates them, it does not branch inside either.
func (s *System) AskDrift(ctx context.Context, question string, opts DriftOptions) (Answer, error)
```

**The composition — primer then local loop then synthesis:**

1. **Primer (global, broad orientation).** Run the v0.8 global path — `selectCommunities` → lazy `communityReports` → map → reduce — to get (a) a primer answer and (b) the set of communities/community-reports that scored highest. The primer's job is *not* the final answer; it is to identify *which entities to drill into*. The highest-scoring communities' member entities become the local loop's seed set. This is one global round: one map call per selected community + one reduce call — the existing `AskGlobal` budget.
2. **Local follow-up loop (entity-anchored drill-down).** For a bounded number of rounds (default **2**, hard cap **3** — mirrors v0.7's KG-7 "hard-bounded traversal" discipline applied to *rounds* instead of hops): each round runs `GraphRetriever`-style retrieval seeded by the current entity set, packs the hits, and prompts `generate.Model` for a partial answer **and a short list of follow-up entities/sub-questions**. Those follow-up entities seed the next round. The loop is the "flexible traversal" — the LLM, not a fixed walk, chooses where to drill.
3. **Termination.** The loop stops on the *first* of: the round cap is hit; the model emits no new follow-up entities (it signals "enough"); or no new entities are reachable. **Bounded by construction** — no unbounded LLM recursion. This is the DRIFT analogue of v0.7's depth cap.
4. **Synthesis (reduce).** One final `generate.Model` call synthesizes the primer answer + every round's partial answer into the final answer — structurally the same as `AskGlobal`'s reduce step.

**LLM round budget (concrete):** primer = (`MaxCommunities` map calls + 1 reduce); local loop = `Rounds` × 1 generation; synthesis = 1. With defaults (`MaxCommunities` 8, `Rounds` 2) that is ~8 + 1 + 2 + 1 ≈ **12 LLM calls** — bounded, predictable, surfaced in `Diagnostics.Drift` and counted by the existing `obs.Counter` (the same counter `AskGlobal` installs at `rag/global.go:69`).

**Graceful degradation.** Like `AskGlobal`, a store with no `CommunityStore` capability, or a namespace with no communities, makes the primer empty — `AskDrift` then degrades to a pure local-loop answer (or an empty answer), no error. Same contract as v0.7's missing-graph-signal and v0.8's missing-community degradation.

**`DriftOptions` stays small** — mirroring `GlobalOptions`'s deliberate minimalism:

```go
type DriftOptions struct {
    Namespace      string
    MaxCommunities int // primer breadth; passthrough to the global primer
    Rounds         int // local follow-up rounds; default 2, hard cap 3
    TopK           int // local-retrieval TopK per round
}
```

## 5. Path-Ranking / Subgraph-as-Evidence

**Question 3 answered.** v0.7's `GraphRetriever` scores *chunks* by node proximity (`math.Pow(decay, hop)` — `retrieve/graph.go:137`). It answers "which chunks are near the query's entities" but never "*how* are entity A and entity B connected" or "what is the evidence path." v0.9 adds, **as an opt-in additive mode on `GraphRetriever`**, deterministic ranking of multi-hop *paths* between linked entities and a structured subgraph surfaced as evidence.

**Deterministic path ranking (pure stdlib, no LLM, no new dependency):**

1. **Path enumeration.** Between the linked seed entities (pairs from `EntityLinker`), enumerate simple paths up to the existing hard depth cap (`maxGraphDepth = 2`, `store/graph.go:14`). Enumeration is a bounded DFS over the `Subgraph` that `Neighborhood` *already returns* — no new store call. Depth 2 + fan-out 64 keeps the path count bounded by construction.
2. **Path scoring.** Score each path deterministically from already-present signals — no randomness, golden-testable:
   - **length** — shorter paths rank higher (a path-length decay, reusing the `HopDecay` idiom);
   - **edge weight** — `Relation.Weight` via the `edgeWeight` convention (`graph/community.go:158`): a path's strength is the product (or min) of its edge weights;
   - **provenance overlap** — a path whose relations share `SourceChunkIDs` (co-attested in the same chunks) is stronger evidence than one stitched across unrelated chunks.
   Combine into one scalar; **tie-break by the sorted entity-ID sequence of the path** so ranking is total and reproducible — the same determinism discipline as `Canonicalize` and `LouvainDetector`.
3. **Structured subgraph-as-evidence return.** The ranked paths plus the entities/relations they traverse form a `graph.Subgraph` (the type already exists, `graph/graph.go:47`) — *the* evidence object. v0.9 adds a thin `RankedPath` type and surfaces both on the trace:

```go
// graph package — additive, near-leaf, stdlib-only.
type RankedPath struct {
    EntityIDs   []string // ordered: path[0] .. path[n], the traversal
    RelationIDs []string // ordered edges between consecutive entities
    Score       float64  // deterministic composite; higher = stronger
}
// PathRanker ranks simple paths within a Subgraph. Deterministic by
// construction: same Subgraph + same seed pairs => same []RankedPath.
type PathRanker interface {
    RankPaths(sub Subgraph, seedPairs [][2]string) []RankedPath
}
```

**Extending `GraphRetriever` / `GraphTrace` without breaking them.** `GraphRetriever` gains an opt-in field (`Paths bool` or a `PathRanker` field — nil/false = v0.7 behavior byte-identical). `GraphTrace` (`retrieve/graph.go:18`) gains two additive fields — exactly how v0.8 added `CommunityIDs`:

```go
type GraphTrace struct {
    SeedEntityIDs    []string
    ReachedEntityIDs []string
    MaxHop           int
    CommunityIDs     []string       // v0.8
    Paths            []graph.RankedPath // v0.9 — nil unless path mode is on
    EvidenceSubgraph *graph.Subgraph    // v0.9 — the structured evidence
}
```

`Retrieve`'s signature, its `[]store.Hit` output, its RRF-fusion behavior, and all v0.7/v0.8 tests are **untouched**. Path ranking is extra *trace* output — a structured-evidence view *alongside* the chunk hits, never a replacement. Chunk hits still drive RRF fusion; the subgraph drives explainability and any caller that wants to render the connection.

## 6. Determinism, Testing & Eval

- **Path ranking** — deterministic by construction (KG4-4): bounded DFS in sorted order, composite score from `Relation.Weight` + length + provenance, total tie-break on the path's entity-ID sequence. Golden-output unit tests like `LouvainDetector`'s and `Canonicalize`'s.
- **DRIFT** — the primer, every local-round generation, and the synthesis call are LLM-backed → scripted-`generate.Model` tests. The orchestration (round count, termination on no-new-entities, seed propagation) is deterministic given a scripted model and *is* golden-testable: a fixed graph + a scripted model with canned per-round responses must yield a fixed round count and a fixed `Diagnostics.Drift`.
- **DRIFT eval** — DRIFT synthesizes an answer with no gold chunk set, exactly like global search. Reuse the `eval.GlobalEvaluator` pattern (`eval/global.go`): a `DriftEvaluator` scoring the Triad's generation legs (groundedness against the consulted reports + per-round local hits; answer relevance) — **not** `RunGraphAB` recall@k.
- **Path-ranking eval** — paths still resolve to chunks, so the *local* path's `eval.RunGraphAB` recall@k still applies for the chunk-hit side. Path *quality* (did the ranked top path actually connect the query entities) wants a small deterministic golden check, not a recall metric.

## Keystone Decisions (for the roadmapper to ratify — continuing the KG / KG3 line)

- **KG4-1 — v0.9 scope is two of v0.8's three deferrals.** v0.9 ships **DRIFT search** and **path-ranking / subgraph-as-evidence**. **Incremental community maintenance is deferred again** — v0.8's full re-detection (KG3-7) stays. Claim/covariate extraction and any graph database remain out of scope.
- **KG4-2 — DRIFT is a third answer path, `System.AskDrift`.** It is a separate method that *orchestrates* the v0.8 global path (`AskGlobal` — the primer) and v0.7's local `GraphRetriever` (the follow-up loop). It does not implement `retrieve.Retriever`; it is not a mode flag on `Ask` or `AskGlobal`. This extends KG3-4 — global search was the second separate answer path; DRIFT is the third.
- **KG4-3 — DRIFT's local loop is hard-bounded.** A round cap (default 2, hard cap 3) plus termination on "model emits no new follow-up entities" or "no new entities reachable". No unbounded LLM recursion — the DRIFT analogue of v0.7's KG-7 depth cap. The total LLM call count is predictable and surfaced in `Diagnostics.Drift`.
- **KG4-4 — Path ranking is deterministic, pure stdlib, and an opt-in additive mode.** A `graph.PathRanker` seam + `RankedPath` type; bounded-DFS path enumeration over the `Subgraph` `Neighborhood` already returns; a composite deterministic score (length + `Relation.Weight` + provenance overlap) with a total tie-break. It is wired as an opt-in field on `retrieve.GraphRetriever`; `GraphTrace` gains additive `Paths` / `EvidenceSubgraph` fields. `GraphRetriever.Retrieve`'s signature, chunk-hit output, and v0.7/v0.8 tests are byte-identical when path mode is off.
- **KG4-5 — Incremental community maintenance is deferred, with a documented trigger.** v0.8's full re-detection on re-ingest is *correct* and fast at SDK scale; incremental Louvain is a large, subtle, second algorithm with a real "incremental == batch" correctness obligation and would need a new `CommunityStore` method. Defer until profiling on a real corpus shows `Detect` dominating re-ingest. Documented in `docs/graphrag.md`.
- **KG4-6 — Determinism is mandatory for every new type.** Path ranking is deterministic by construction (golden-tested). DRIFT's LLM-backed steps are scripted-`generate.Model` tested; its orchestration (round count, termination, seed propagation) is golden-tested against a scripted model. `Diagnostics.Drift` is deterministic given a scripted model.
- **KG4-7 — No new dependency; no existing seam changes shape.** DRIFT is orchestration over `AskGlobal` + `GraphRetriever` + `generate.Model`. Path ranking is a stdlib graph computation over the existing `Subgraph`. `AskGlobal`, `GraphRetriever`, `GraphStore`, `CommunityStore`, `LouvainDetector` are all untouched. v0.9 holds the stdlib + `postgres`-island line, exactly as v0.7 and v0.8 did.

## Gaps / Open Questions for the Roadmapper

- **`AskDrift` reusing `AskGlobal` — call the method or factor the internals?** `AskDrift` could call `s.AskGlobal` directly (simplest; double `obs.Counter` install is harmless) or share an unexported `globalPrimer` helper so the primer's consulted communities flow into the local loop without re-deriving them. Lean toward the shared helper — the primer needs the *selected communities*, not just the answer text. A Phase-27 plan-time call.
- **Path mode field shape on `GraphRetriever`** — a `bool Paths` (ranker is internal/default) vs an injectable `PathRanker` field (nil = off). The injectable field matches the repo's seam discipline (`EntityLinker`, `CommunityDetector`); recommend that. Phase-26 decision.
- **`EvidenceSubgraph` ownership** — whether the structured evidence lives on `GraphTrace` (retrieve-layer) only, or is also promoted onto `rag.Answer`/`Diagnostics` for `Ask` callers. Recommend `GraphTrace` first; promote to `Diagnostics` only if a caller needs it.
- **DRIFT local loop — reuse `GraphRetriever` or `Ask`'s full pipeline per round?** A round could call bare `GraphRetriever.Retrieve` (lean) or the full retrieve→rerank→pack path. Lean bare `GraphRetriever` per round — DRIFT supplies its own synthesis; rerank/pack would double the machinery. Phase-27 decision.
- **Path scoring weights** — the relative weighting of length vs `Relation.Weight` vs provenance overlap is a tuning knob. Ship conservative, documented defaults; expose nothing until a real corpus motivates a knob — the `GlobalOptions`-minimalism precedent.
